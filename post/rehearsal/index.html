Rehearsal of Life

Einmal ist keinmal, says Tomas to himself. What happens but once, says the German adage, might as well not have happened at all. If we have only one life to live, we might as well not have lived at all.

However, the long long history of our imperial past, told us to live in a scenario once and once more won’t bring us any further either. When everyone on this land forgets about Robespierre, he will certainly return, sometimes even with cheers of joy, he again, cuts off tens of thousand heads on this land, sometimes even with joy, like a show that forever is at rehearsal with then silent audiences. There is, however, not much innocence in this imposed silence of imposed darkness.

It seems that mortality is a gift, as it shows that life is merely a rehearsal, it encourages us and forces us to live bravely. It anaesthetizes us, makes us forget that the daunting ghost of the old system is actually not far away, only a rehearsal called the great revolution is between this ghost and us.
